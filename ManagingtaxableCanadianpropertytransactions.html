Managing taxable Canadian property transactions

Individual Tax Number (ITN)

ITN applications should be submitted separately from your disposition request to avoid delays. To apply, follow the instructions found at Applying for an Individual Taxation Number or review the section Do I need a taxation number? on this page.

As a non-resident of Canada, you have to follow certain procedures if you have disposed of, or are planning to dispose of certain types of Canadian property.

Under section 116 of the Income Tax Act, non resident vendors (from now on referred to as vendors) who dispose of certain taxable Canadian property have to notify the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) about the disposition either before they dispose of the property or within ten days after the disposition. When the CRA has received either an amount to cover the tax on any gain the vendor may realize upon the disposition of property, or appropriate security for the tax, the CRA will issue a certificate of compliance to the vendor.
